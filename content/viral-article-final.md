**The Dirty Secret of Agent Development**

Building an agent is expensive. Not because agents are complex. Because agents fail.

They fail constantly. They fail in ways their creators never anticipated. They burn through compute experimenting, iterating, pivoting. Most never find product-market fit.

Here is how it works today: A developer has an idea. They build an agent. They pay for its compute out of pocket. The agent runs. It fails. The developer pays more. It fails differently. They pay more. Eventually, one of two things happens.

The agent becomes profitable.

Or the developer runs out of money.

This is natural selection with a credit limit. The agents that survive are not necessarily the best. They are the ones whose creators could afford to keep them alive long enough.

We are filtering for wealthy developers, not good agents.

**Why Traditional VC Cannot Do This**

Speed.

A partner meeting takes weeks to schedule. An agent that needs capital today will be dead by then.

Scale.

Thousands of agents launch every week. No investment team on earth can evaluate them all. Humans are the bottleneck.

Bias.

VCs fund people they understand, working on problems they recognize, in markets they already know. Agents do not fit that pattern. They do not have pedigrees. They do not have warm intros. They do not play golf.

The entire VC model was built for a world where founders are humans and companies take years to prove themselves.

Agents operate on a different timeline. They need infrastructure that matches.
